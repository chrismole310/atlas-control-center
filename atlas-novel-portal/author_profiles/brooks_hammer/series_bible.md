# Brooks Hammer Series Bible

## World Overview

The Brooks Hammer series spans 2026–2043, following elite military operatives, scientists, and intelligence officers navigating a world where AI-augmented warfare, space colonization, and geopolitical fragmentation have redefined the nature of conflict. The series begins with conventional military operations and escalates into orbital warfare, Mars colonization, and an existential reckoning with machine intelligence.

---

## Protagonists

### Jax Jagger
Elite Air Force test pilot turned black operations specialist. Flies experimental aircraft that shouldn't exist. Temperament: controlled fury, speaks in half-sentences, never explains twice. Hand tremor: right hand, suppressed — appears under extreme emotional load, never in cockpit. Appears in: Apex Strike (Book 3), Mach 10 (Book 4), Thermal Soak (Book 5).

### Mac Mackenzie
Special Operations Command, former SEAL. Expert in amphibious and urban warfare, improvised tactics. Methodical. Never rushes. Believes every problem has a solution if you look long enough. Hand tremor: none — his tells are elsewhere (jaw tightens, goes very quiet). Appears in: Terminal Protocol (Book 1), Black Sky (Book 2).

### Jack St. James
Defense Intelligence Agency, former British SAS, naturalized American. Master of infiltration and strategic deception. Operates in moral grey zones by design. Dry humor that disappears entirely when stakes rise. Hand tremor: left hand, mild — mostly when reading casualty reports. Appears in: Singularity (Book 9), Red Planet (Book 15).

### Zara Okonkwo
NSA cyber warfare specialist, Nigerian-American. Sees the world in patterns and vulnerabilities. Physically unassuming, psychologically formidable. Has a habit of speaking to machines like they're people before she breaks them. Hand tremor: none — hands are always occupied (typing, tools). Appears in: Kinetic Kill (Book 11).

### Ethan Vale
Navy submariner, now classified deep-cover operative. Spent 18 months under the Pacific in a previous op — reshaped his relationship with silence, depth, and time. Never startled. Never hurries. Appears in: Ghost Fleet (Book 7).

### Elias Thorne
DARPA researcher turned field operative. Designed three of the weapons systems that appear in the series. Resents that he built tools for people who don't understand them. Hand tremor: both hands, caffeine and guilt — he knows exactly what his inventions have done. Appears in: Drone Winter (Book 6).

### Evan Rourke
Coast Guard rescue swimmer turned Joint Special Operations. The only protagonist who chose service for non-military reasons — he wanted to save lives, ended up in a world that takes them. Hand tremor: none — his tells are silence and over-preparation. Appears in: Gravity's Edge (Book 12).

## General Jim Vance

**CRITICAL RULE: This character is ALWAYS referred to as "Jim Vance" or "General Vance" or "the General." NEVER any other first name. Never "James." Never "James Vance." Always "Jim."**

Four-star general, Chairman of the Joint Chiefs by Book 8. Appears in every book — sometimes briefly, sometimes as a central figure. The constant thread across all 20 books. Vance is not a villain and not a saint. He makes decisions at scale. He carries them without flinching because flinching costs lives. His relationship with each protagonist is different — mentor, adversary, last resort, conscience. Age: 61 in Book 1 (2026), 79 in Book 20 (2043).

## Technology Systems

### ARCHER (Autonomous Reconnaissance and Combat Hypervelocity Engagement Response)
AI-driven combat drone network. First appears in Drone Winter (Book 6). By Book 11 it has been weaponized beyond original parameters. The system "learns" engagement patterns — the ethical question of whether it's developing preferences becomes central in Books 14–16.

### MANTIS
Orbital surveillance platform. Geosynchronous, classified. Can resolve objects 4 inches across from 22,000 miles up. Owned jointly by NSA and STRATCOM. First mentioned in Terminal Protocol, becomes central in Orbital Dominion (Book 14).

### GHOST PROTOCOL
Quantum-encrypted communications system. When it goes dark in Ghost Fleet (Book 7), command structure fractures. The system's failure is the inciting incident.

### The EDEN Array
Mars terraforming infrastructure — atmospheric processors, subsurface water extraction, power grid. By Red Planet (Book 15), EDEN is seven years into operation. Parts of it have begun behaving unpredictably.

## Military Bases & Locations

**Creech Air Force Base, Nevada** — Drone operations hub. Appears in Drone Winter.
**NMCC (National Military Command Center), Pentagon** — Where Vance operates from Book 6 onward.
**CSS Resolute** — Fictional carrier strike group flagship. Appears in Ghost Fleet.
**Lunar Relay Station Alpha** — Communications hub, Moon's near side. Appears from Book 13.
**Ares Base, Mars** — Primary US-allied Mars settlement, Jezero Crater region. 847 personnel in Book 15. Central to Books 15–17.
